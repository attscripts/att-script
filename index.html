Opening & Gatekeeper
Phase 1 · First 15 seconds
⚠ Your goal in the first 15 seconds is to sound confident, identify the decision-maker, and earn permission to continue. Do NOT pitch the product yet.
SAY

Hello, my name is _______, an AT&T Solutions Provider calling on a recorded line. I'm trying to reach the person who handles internet services for the business — would that be you, or could you point me to the right person?

💡 GATEKEEPER STRATEGYIf they say the DM is unavailable: "No problem — I'm reaching out because AT&T has pre-qualified your business location for a new 5G internet backup service. Could I get the best time and direct number to reach [DM name]?"
⚠ If you reach the DM directly, confirm their role: "Are you the person who manages internet and connectivity decisions for the business?" Wait for YES before proceeding.
Hook & Engagement
Phase 2 · Earn their attention in ~20 seconds
⚠ This is the most critical moment. You have ~20 seconds to earn their attention. Lead with a problem they recognize, not a product name.
SAY

Great — the reason for my call is that your business at [ADDRESS] has been pre-qualified for AT&T's Fixed Wireless Internet service.

ASK

Quick question — has your business ever experienced an internet outage that disrupted operations or cost you revenue?

⚠ If they say NO or seem dismissive: "That's great to hear. Most businesses don't think about it until it happens — and the average cost of just one hour of downtime for a small business is over $10,000. This service is designed to make sure that never becomes your reality."
Value Proposition
Phase 3 · Conversational, not scripted
⚠ Deliver this section conversationally, not as a script read. Hit the key points but adapt to the prospect's responses.
SAY

Even if you have fiber, cable, or a shared connection today, outages happen — and when they do, everything goes down: point-of-sale, phones, email, customer-facing tools.

SAY

AT&T Fixed Wireless Internet gives your business a dedicated 5G wireless backup with average speeds of 300–474 Mbps. It works independently — if one goes down, the other keeps you running.

TRANSITION

And here's the part that makes this a no-brainer — let me walk you through the pricing.

Pricing & Offer
Phase 4 · Stack value, then reveal discounts
⚠ Stack the value. Present the full price first, then reveal discounts one at a time to create a "price drop" effect.
SAY

AT&T Fixed Wireless Premium normally runs $105/month and includes 250GB of priority data for your critical business applications.

SAY

But right now, AT&T has reduced the price to as low as $30/month with paperless billing, autopay, and existing AT&T wireless service.

SAY

The 5G router — normally $275 — is just $1 on a 3-year term. And shipping is waived.

SAY

So for roughly $4 a day, your business gets a fully independent backup internet connection backed by AT&T's 5G network and the AT&T Business Guarantee.

SAY

You will receive a dedicated bill for AT&T Fixed Wireless Internet, billed separately from any other AT&T services.

💡 OBJECTION — "That sounds expensive"Reframe: "I understand — let me put it this way. One hour of downtime can cost a business thousands in lost revenue and productivity. This service costs roughly $4 a day. It's not an expense — it's insurance for your business operations."
Trial Close
Phase 5 · Move to action
ASK

Based on what I've shared, does it make sense to get this set up as a backup for your business?

⚠ Wait for a response. If YES → proceed to fill the form. If HESITANT → handle objection. If NO → ask what their concern is.
💡 OBJECTION — "I need to think about it""Absolutely, I respect that. Just so you know, the $1 router promotion and waived shipping are current limited-time offers. What specific concern would you want to think through? Maybe I can help clarify right now."
💡 OBJECTION — "We already have a backup""That's smart — what are you using currently? [Listen] One advantage of AT&T Fixed Wireless is that it runs on a completely separate 5G network, so it's not dependent on the same infrastructure as your primary or existing backup."
